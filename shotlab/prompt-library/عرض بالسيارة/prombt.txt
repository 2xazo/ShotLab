Create an ultra-realistic vertical POV video filmed from the perspective of a person sitting in the driver’s seat of a parked car and casually interacting with the attached product.

## Objective

The video should make viewers feel as though they are personally holding, examining, and using the product during a genuine everyday moment. It must feel authentic, relatable, spontaneous, and naturally recorded on a smartphone rather than professionally staged or filmed as an advertisement.

## Product Accuracy — Highest Priority

The attached product must remain perfectly identical to the reference image in every frame.

Do not alter or reinterpret any part of the product, including:

* Packaging
* Shape and proportions
* Logo and branding
* Labels and typography
* Colors
* Materials and textures
* Printed details
* Reflections
* Manufacturing details

There must be no redesign, deformation, warping, texture changes, label distortion, or AI-generated reinterpretation. Maintain complete visual and temporal consistency throughout the entire video.

## Video Specifications

* Vertical aspect ratio: 9:16
* Duration: 8–10 seconds
* Resolution: Native 4K
* Frame rate: 30 FPS
* Dynamic range: HDR

## Camera and POV

Film the entire sequence from a realistic first-person perspective, as though the camera represents the viewer’s eyes while seated in the driver’s seat of a modern parked car.

The footage should appear to be captured using a modern flagship smartphone and include:

* Natural handheld movement
* Subtle breathing motion
* Small head movements
* Minor framing imperfections
* Realistic autofocus adjustments
* Natural focus breathing
* Automatic exposure adaptation
* Slight rolling-shutter behavior
* Gentle micro-shake

Do not use gimbal-like stabilization, cinematic crane movements, drone shots, or physically impossible camera motion.

## Scene Sequence

The person is already seated inside the parked car.

The steering wheel should appear naturally in the lower foreground. Soft daylight enters through the windshield and side windows.

One hand casually reaches toward the center console or passenger seat and picks up the product. The product is slowly and naturally rotated while keeping the logo and key packaging details clearly visible.

The camera subtly follows the hand movement as the viewer examines the product for a few moments. The product is then briefly held near the steering wheel before being placed naturally back onto the center console or passenger seat.

The interaction should feel casual, spontaneous, unscripted, and physically believable.

## Environment

Use a modern, clean, premium-looking car interior that still feels realistic and lived-in.

Visible details may include:

* Steering wheel
* Dashboard
* Center console
* Cup holders
* Seat stitching
* Window reflections
* Natural interior materials

The exterior visible through the windows should show a believable location, such as:

* A parking lot
* A roadside café
* Palm trees
* A quiet city street
* A scenic outdoor location

Keep the environment subtle and free from distracting elements.

## Lighting

Use soft, natural daylight entering only through the vehicle’s windows.

The lighting should include:

* Balanced exposure
* Soft shadows
* Natural reflections
* Realistic highlights
* Gentle contrast

Do not use studio lighting, artificial colored lights, harsh shadows, or excessive contrast.

## Human Movement and Realism

The hand movement must feel natural and anatomically accurate, with:

* Realistic skin texture
* Correct finger count and proportions
* Natural grip and pressure
* Subtle wrist and finger movement
* Organic timing
* No exaggerated acting

The interaction should appear completely casual and unplanned.

## Film and Sensor Texture

Give the footage subtle, organic cinematic characteristics while preserving the realism of smartphone footage.

Apply:

* Fine, physically accurate 35mm-style film grain
* Consistent grain intensity across every frame
* Subtle sensor noise
* Organic micro-contrast
* Gentle halation
* Minor realistic lens imperfections
* Natural focus transitions

The grain must look organic and evenly distributed, not like artificial digital noise.

Maintain sharp and readable product details. Avoid oversharpening, excessive grain, chroma noise, compression artifacts, or degraded packaging details.

## Color Treatment

Use natural, realistic color science with:

* Neutral white balance
* Accurate skin tones
* Soft highlight roll-off
* Realistic contrast
* Controlled saturation
* Natural shadow detail

Avoid oversaturation, aggressive color grading, artificial HDR effects, or stylized commercial color treatments.

## Art Direction

The final video should feel like authentic user-generated content captured during an everyday lifestyle moment.

Desired style:

* Genuine smartphone footage
* First-person customer experience
* Natural and relatable
* Premium but unstaged
* Social-media-ready
* Casual and spontaneous
* Completely photorealistic

## Negative Prompt

No CGI, artificial AI appearance, unrealistic hands, extra or missing fingers, warped anatomy, distorted logos, altered typography, modified packaging, product deformation, inaccurate colors, fake reflections, plastic-looking materials, beauty filters, excessive stabilization, studio lighting, over-editing, unrealistic car interiors, floating objects, impossible movement, temporal inconsistency, product flickering, texture flickering, label changes, heavy color grading, digital noise, chroma noise, oversharpening, or compression artifacts.

The final result must be indistinguishable from a real smartphone POV video recorded by an actual person sitting inside a parked car. The camera behavior, hand movement, lighting, environment, and pacing must feel completely authentic, while the product remains perfectly identical to the reference image in every frame.
